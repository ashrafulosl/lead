# 90-Day Plan: Building the Physique and Presence of a Principled Leader
**Start date:** July 10, 2026 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **End date:** October 8, 2026

---

## The Anchor

> *"I am building the physique, presence, and character of a principled entrepreneur — competent, trustworthy, educated, quietly ambitious."*

Everything below serves this identity, not a number on a scale or a bicep measurement. Physical training is the fastest-moving lever, so it's the primary focus these 90 days — but it's built on the same discipline that will carry into knowledge and character work.

This plan has four tracks that run in parallel every week:
1. **Physique** — training + nutrition
2. **Appearance** — grooming, skin, clothing
3. **Presence** — posture, communication, body language
4. **Character** — reading, reflection, tied to your Mental Board

---

## Track 1: Physique

### Phase 1 physical goals (this 90 days)
- Add 2–3 kg of lean mass
- Improve posture
- Broaden shoulders
- Grow arms
- Reduce belly slightly
- Increase daily energy

### Weekly routine (Home Edition — no gym required)

Fits around your evening work shift — train **before work**, roughly 45–60 minutes. Rest 60–90 seconds between sets.

**Equipment:** none required. A sturdy backpack loaded with books/water bottles works as your adjustable weight for presses, rows, and RDLs. A pull-up bar (doorframe bar, ~1,500–3,000 BDT) is the one purchase worth making — it unlocks your whole pull day. A resistance band is optional and cheap if you want it.

**Day 1 (Mon) — Push: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | At home |
|---|---|---|
| Push-ups | 3 × 12–15 | Floor |
| Pike push-ups | 3 × 8–12 | Floor, feet elevated on a chair for more difficulty |
| Backpack shoulder press | 3 × 10–12 | Loaded backpack pressed overhead, one or both arms |
| Chair dips | 3 × 10–12 | Between two sturdy chairs or a bed edge |
| Backpack lateral raises | 3 × 12–15 | Light backpack or water bottles in each hand |

**Day 2 (Tue) — Pull: Back, Biceps, Forearms**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | At home |
|---|---|---|
| Pull-ups / chin-ups | 3 × 6–10 | Doorframe pull-up bar (best investment for this plan) |
| Inverted rows | 3 × 10–12 | Under a sturdy table, or low bar — feet on floor, pull chest to edge |
| Backpack rows | 3 × 10–12 | Bent-over row holding loaded backpack in each hand |
| Resistance band rows | 3 × 12–15 | Band anchored to a door hinge or bar, if you have one |
| Backpack curls | 3 × 10–15 | Loaded backpack or water bottles, one or both arms |
| Hammer curls | 3 × 10–15 | Water bottles held vertically (neutral grip) |

*No pull-up bar yet? Substitute with backpack rows + band rows only until you get one — pull-ups are worth prioritizing once you can.*

**Day 3 (Wed) — Rest**
Walk, stretch, read, recover. No equipment.

**Day 4 (Thu) — Legs**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | At home |
|---|---|---|
| Squats | 3 × 12–15 | Bodyweight, or backpack held at chest for added load |
| Romanian deadlifts | 3 × 10–12 | Loaded backpack held in front, hinge at hips |
| Walking lunges | 3 × 10/leg | Down a hallway or room, bodyweight |
| Calf raises | 3 × 15–20 | Standing on a stair edge for full range |
| Plank | 3 × 30–45 sec | Floor |

**Day 5 (Fri) — Upper Body (full push+pull) + Direct Arm Work**
| Exercise | Sets × Reps | At home |
|---|---|---|
| Pull-ups | 3 × 6–10 | Doorframe bar |
| Push-ups | 3 × 12–15 | Floor |
| Backpack rows | 3 × 10–12 | Bent-over, as above |
| Backpack shoulder press | 3 × 10–12 | As above |
| Backpack curls | 3 × 10–15 | As above |
| Hammer curls | 3 × 10–15 | Water bottles |
| Chair dips | 3 × 10–15 | Between two chairs |

*Triceps make up roughly two-thirds of upper arm size — don't skip them even though biceps feel more exciting.*

**Day 6 (Sat) — Light**
30-minute walk outside or around your building, mobility, stretching. No equipment.

**Day 7 (Sun) — Complete rest**

**Progressive overload rule at home:** since you can't easily add small weight increments like a gym, your main levers are **reps, sets, and tempo** — add one rep per exercise per week, or slow the movement down (3 seconds lowering) once bodyweight reps get easy. Once your backpack gets too light even fully loaded, that's your sign to look into a cheap adjustable dumbbell set. Example: Week 1 = 10 push-ups → Week 2 = 11 → Week 3 = 12. Small and boring weekly, but it compounds hard over a year. Write your numbers down; if you don't track it, you can't tell if you're progressing.

**Priority order for visual impact** (train these hardest): shoulders → upper back → chest → triceps → forearms → legs. Biceps get direct work but aren't the priority — triceps and shoulders will change your silhouette faster.

### Nutrition (daily targets)

- **Protein:** 100–120 g/day *(appropriate target given your current bodyweight of ~63–65 kg)*
- **Calories:** small surplus (~200–300 kcal above maintenance)
- **Base foods:** rice, eggs, chicken, fish, milk, lentils, vegetables, banana, peanuts
- No expensive supplements needed — whole food covers this

**Simple daily template:**
- Breakfast: 3 eggs + milk
- Lunch: rice + chicken/fish + vegetables
- Snack: banana + peanuts
- Dinner: rice + beef/fish/chicken + vegetables
- Before bed: milk

### Recovery
- Sleep target: 7.5–9 hours (adjust around your 6 PM–2 AM work schedule — protect a consistent sleep window even if it's shifted later)
- One equipment note: an adjustable dumbbell set (10–20 kg total) will meaningfully speed things up if the budget allows. A loaded backpack is a solid substitute in the meantime.

## Track 3: Presence

### Posture (highest ROI, costs nothing)
Practice daily until automatic: shoulders back, chest open, neck neutral, deliberate unhurried walk. This alone changes how you're perceived before any muscle is gained.

### Communication practice
- Speak at a calm, measured pace
- Use short, clear sentences — resist over-explaining
- Get comfortable with pauses instead of filling silence
- Hold eye contact naturally
- Practice listening fully before responding

A simple weekly habit: pick one conversation each week (work or personal) to consciously apply these in, then briefly reflect afterward on what worked.
